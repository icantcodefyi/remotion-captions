# MeowCap — Design Context

## Product

MeowCap is a browser-based caption studio. A creator drops a video in, the app
transcribes with Deepgram (or aligns their own script), and renders a Remotion
preview in one of eight caption styles (TikTok, Hormozi, Beast, Karaoke,
Minimal, Neon, Typewriter, Broadcast). Output is .srt / .json.

The Remotion caption styles inside videos are intentionally loud — that is the
product. The **app UI around them** is the opposite: quiet, confident, crafted,
so the styles themselves can sing.

## Users

Short-form creators (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) and indie filmmakers who care about
how their captions look. They are visual people. They judge tools by feel
within five seconds.

Primary context: sitting at a desk late at night or between shoots, one-off
clip in hand, wants captions they wouldn't be embarrassed to ship.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **crafty, confident, playful-but-grown-up.**

Not cute. Not corporate. Not a dev tool. Closer to an independent print
magazine's website than to a SaaS dashboard.

Voice: warm and direct. Says what things do, not what they are. Doesn't
explain jokes; trusts the reader.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Warm editorial paper.** Cream / off-white surfaces in light mode, tinted
warm near-black in dark mode. A single lime accent that earns its rarity.
Serif numerals for data (timestamps, file sizes, durations) — the one
editorial flourish that separates this from every other AI tool.

### Typography

- **Display** (masthead, hero, big numbers): **Bricolage Grotesque** —
  variable font with a grade axis, distinctive but not "designy."
- **Body + UI** (labels, buttons, body text): **Host Grotesk** — quiet,
  well-metric'd, great at 13-16px.
- **Serif numerals & italic labels** (stats, timestamps, inline emphasis):
  **Spectral** — a Google serif with real editorial weight. Used sparingly.
- **Mono** (API key field, code-like values): **JetBrains Mono**.

Banned: Inter, Geist, DM Sans/Serif, Plus Jakarta, Outfit, Instrument,
Fraunces, Playfair, Cormorant, Syne, Space Grotesk/Mono, IBM Plex.

### Color

- OKLCH throughout. No HSL, no raw hex except for the caption-style preview
  defaults (those live in the video, not the UI).
- Brand: lime around hue 128.
- Neutrals tinted ~0.008 chroma toward hue 90 (warm paper, not cool gray).
- Pure black and pure white are forbidden.

### Theme

**Both light and dark, with a manual toggle** (respects `prefers-color-scheme`
on first load). Light is the default personality; dark is for late-night
sessions.

## Anti-references

Do not look like:
- Generic AI app — **no** gradient text, no purple→cyan glows, no glassmorphism.
- Big-tech SaaS dashboard — no Stripe/Intercom/Segment card grids.
- Cartoon / Notion template — no bubbly illustrations, no dancing emojis.
- Dev-tool severity — no all-monospace, no terminal green, no hacker energy.

## Design Principles

1. **Paper, not plastic.** Surfaces feel warm and tangible. Shadows are
   subtle; borders carry more of the hierarchy.
2. **Quiet until it matters.** The app is mostly neutral; the accent appears
   in maybe three places on screen. When it appears, it means something.
3. **Text is the product.** Since the tool styles captions, the tool's
   typography must itself be the showcase piece. Numerals are serif for
   exactly this reason.
4. **Motion as punctuation.** Fade-rise on mount, gentle lift on hover,
   nothing bouncy. Respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
5. **One accent, no exceptions.** Lime for state, focus, CTAs, selection.
   Never purple, never a secondary accent in the chrome.
